Predictive Shot Selection Engine

A hybrid deep learning pipeline that forecasts a cricket batsman's intended shot type — Drive, Pull, or Cut — before the ball reaches the crease, by fusing real-time object tracking with spatial heatmap analysis.

Role

ML Engineer

Timeline

4 months

Stack

Python, YOLO, U-Net, PyTorch, OpenCV

Problem

Cricket coaching and broadcast analytics rely on post-hoc shot classification — labeling shots after they've been played. There was no system capable of predicting shot intent before impact, which would be invaluable for bowler strategy, coaching feedback, and real-time broadcast overlays.

Approach

The pipeline operates in two parallel streams that are fused before prediction:

  • Tracking stream: YOLO detects and tracks the ball, bat, and batsman across consecutive frames, encoding trajectory vectors (speed, angle, bounce point).
  • Spatial stream: A U-Net backbone generates per-frame heatmaps of the crease region, encoding the batsman's weight distribution, stance shift, and foot movement patterns.
  • Fusion: Both feature sets feed into a lightweight temporal classifier (GRU) that outputs shot-type probabilities 200–400ms before ball contact.

Outcome

The model achieved 78% top-1 accuracy across Drive, Pull, and Cut categories on a test set of 1,200 deliveries from professional match footage. Prediction latency was under 50ms per frame on a single GPU, enabling real-time overlay during live broadcasts.

Key Technologies

  • YOLO — Multi-object tracking (ball, bat, batsman)
  • U-Net — Crease-region spatial heatmap extraction
  • GRU — Temporal fusion and shot classification
  • PyTorch — Model training and inference
  • OpenCV — Video preprocessing and frame extraction
