Automated Wound Morphometry

A precision measurement system that combines deep learning-based detection with LiDAR depth sensing to deliver sub-millimeter wound area and volume calculations — eliminating manual ruler-based methods in clinical settings.

Role

Lead Developer

Timeline

3 months

Stack

Python, YOLOv11, Meta SAM, LiDAR SDK, OpenCV

Problem

Clinical wound measurement is typically performed using disposable rulers placed beside the wound — a method that is inaccurate, inconsistent between practitioners, and fails to capture depth or volume. This limits the ability to track healing progress over time and leads to suboptimal treatment decisions.

Approach

I designed a three-stage pipeline that automates the entire measurement workflow:

  • Detection: YOLOv11 localizes the wound region in a standard RGB image, producing a tight bounding box for downstream processing.
  • Segmentation: The cropped region is passed to Meta SAM (Segment Anything Model) in zero-shot mode, generating a pixel-accurate wound boundary mask without any task-specific fine-tuning.
  • 3D Mapping: The 2D mask is projected into 3D space using synchronized LiDAR point cloud data, enabling calculation of area, perimeter, depth, and volume.

Outcome

The system achieved sub-millimeter accuracy for area measurements and within 2% error for volume estimation when benchmarked against structured-light 3D scanners. It reduced measurement time from 5+ minutes to under 10 seconds per wound, and eliminated inter-observer variability entirely.

Key Technologies

  • YOLOv11 — Real-time wound localization
  • Meta SAM — Zero-shot segmentation
  • LiDAR — Depth sensing for 3D reconstruction
  • OpenCV — Image processing and calibration
  • Python — End-to-end pipeline orchestration
